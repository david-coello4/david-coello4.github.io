Minecraft 3D Print Art

Created in 2024

Minecraft is a popular game where the user has the ability to create anything using a variety of blocks and their imagination. I planned to start a project where I used Minecraft as a software to create a structure, then export the structure code into a 3D printing software, and finally 3D print the structure in real life. To start this project, I thought about what structure I wanted to build, then came to the conclusion of the characters of Spongebob and Patrick. Instead of a normal scaled version of both characters, I used a scene from The SpongeBob Movie where Patrick is giant compared to Spongebob, and built both into Minecraft, as seen on the left photo below.

Once I had my structure finished, I extracted the files from Minecraft and transferred them into a software called FlashPrint 5 that creates a model of your desired structure before the 3D printing process. The right image below showcases the Minecraft model on the FlashPrint 5 software before printing.


Before printing the entire structure, I wanted to make sure that it printed correctly without any issues, so I printed a draft version of just SpongeBob seen on the left image below. After making sure that the draft print was a success without issues, I finally imported the entire Minecraft structure, and printed both SpongeBob and Patrick, seen on the right image below.

Unfortunately, the SpongeBob structure apart of the Patrick build broke off because of the weak supports, so I had to use the first drafted SpongeBob as a replacement. After everything, I painted each with their appropriate colors and finished my 3D printed structure.
